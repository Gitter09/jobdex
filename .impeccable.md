## Design Context

### Users
VC researchers, startup founders, job seekers, and professional networkers who treat relationships as assets. They use JobDex during focused work sessions — tracking outreach, following up on deals, managing their personal network. They value privacy and own their data. They're technical-adjacent: comfortable with keyboard shortcuts, power-user patterns, dense information layouts. The app is a personal instrument, not a team tool.

### Brand Personality
**Calm, Focused, Powerful.** JobDex is a quiet instrument, not a dashboard. It doesn't announce itself — it lets the work speak. Personality is indie and honest: built by one person for real use, not designed by committee. It respects the user's intelligence and gets out of the way.

### Aesthetic Direction

#### App UI (src/)
**Dark mode first.** The primary experience is dark, depth-layered, and precise.

References:
- **Linear** — precision, speed, dark surfaces, high information density with total clarity
- **Obsidian** — local-first intimacy, personal tool feel, dark and quiet
- **Attio** — modern CRM data density, clean record views, structured without being sterile

Anti-references: Salesforce (corporate, overwhelming), HubSpot (marketing-y, enterprise bloat), generic SaaS (gradient abuse, confetti onboarding).

Color approach: Near-black backgrounds with subtle depth layering. Default accent is **Neutral** (`#222326` — Linear's Nordic gray): monochromatic hierarchy using shades of gray/black rather than a hue. Users can override this in Settings → Appearance. No decorative glows or gradients — contrast and layering do the work. Typography is crisp and functional at small sizes (tables are the primary surface).

#### Marketing Site (docs/)
**Linear palette + Attio structure.** Drop the current green/space aesthetic entirely.

Linear brand colors:
- Base background: `#000000` (pure black)
- Surface / elevated: `#222326` (Nordic gray — Linear's brand color)
- Primary text: `#F4F5F8` (Mercury white)
- Secondary text: ~60% opacity Mercury white
- Tertiary text: ~35% opacity Mercury white
- Borders: ~8–10% white opacity (subtle, not heavy)

Design language:
- Linear's precision: tight typography, restrained use of color, no decorative gradients
- Attio's structure: clean section hierarchy, well-proportioned cards, data-forward feature previews
- No neon glows, no animated stars/nebula, no bright green accent
- Subtle surface differentiation through opacity and layering, not color contrast
- App preview mockups remain but styled to match the new dark neutral palette

### Design Principles

1. **Clarity before decoration.** Every pixel either helps the user do their work or it doesn't belong. When in doubt, remove it.

2. **Dark, layered, never flat.** Use 3–4 depth levels (background → surface → elevated → overlay) to create hierarchy without heavy borders. Let contrast do the work.

3. **Data deserves respect.** Tables and lists are the primary UI surface. Treat them like instruments: readable at a glance, scannable, actionable without drilling down. Density is a feature, not a problem.

4. **Restraint signals capability.** The most powerful tools are the quietest. Animations and accent color must earn their place. One moment of craft is worth more than ten decorative accents.

5. **It feels like yours.** Local-first and private should be *felt* in the UI, not just stated in copy. The experience should feel personal, not like a SaaS product. Customization (accent color, dark mode) reinforces ownership.
